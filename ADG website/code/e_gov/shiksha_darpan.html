E-shiksha

e-SHIKSHA is one of our most unique initiatives that provides computer education to disadvantaged youth and school students. The project was initiated in the year 2008 in one of the remotest part of the Guna district in Madhya Pradesh. In its preliminary stages, it involved equipping 2 vans with power generators, about 25 notebook computers and a qualified instructor moving from one village to another on a scheduled roster to provide a 6-month IT course to students and drop-out youths alike. The van entering a village was often an object of awe and curiosity for people who had never seen a computer. From this very humble beginning, we have expanded the e-SHIKSHA program to the poorest parts of some of India’s largest metropolises. The course offered is endorsed by UNICEF and approved by Ministry of HRD, Government of India.

“There is no computer or computer course within at least a 25 kilometre radius of where I live. We have no electricity supply in this village. To have E- SHIKSHA van scheduled to come to our village and offer courses for free is a sign of hope for us. I can now look for employment with the help of this course and improve the household income.” The Project is being carried out in phase manner for a period of 3 years with extension for 2 years.
